Key Events, Policies, and Disruptions in Pakistan (2016‑2018) Impacting Online Sales

Sep 28‑30, 2016 – Pakistan’s Great Online Shopping Festival 2016
- A nationwide event with discounts and e‑commerce partners. Boosted awareness of online shopping, increased traffic and volume during those days.

Nov 25, 2016 – Daraz Black Friday 2016 (“Pakistan’s first one billion rupee sale”)
- Huge milestone sale day. Increased consumer expectation of discounts, pressure on logistics and inventory, boosted adoption of e‑commerce.

Dec 26, 2016 – PTCL Internet / Cellular Outage
- Large outage across Sindh, Balochistan, Punjab (~5 hours). Disrupted internet and mobile services. Direct impact on online sales, payments, orders during outage.

July 1, 2017 – New 0.5% Minimum Tax for Online Marketplaces
- Finance Act 2017 introduced special provisions: “online marketplaces” subject to concessional minimum tax of 0.5% under section 113. Extra compliance burden, affects profitability.

Late 2017 – Finance Act 2017 Definitions & Tax Adjustments
- Defined “online marketplace.” Reduced turnover tax from 1.25% to 0.5%. Commission‑brokerage tax set at 5% (advance) for online marketplaces. Slight relief, but still compliance costs.

June 2018 – Third Devaluation of Pakistani Rupee in FY18
- PKR devalued multiple times in FY18. Raised cost of imports, reducing affordability of imported goods online. Increased consumer prices, squeezed seller margins.

2016‑2018 (Various) – Internet / Connectivity Disruptions
- Multiple outages (major one Dec 2016). Reduced consumer trust, directly disrupted ordering and payment systems.

2016‑2018 (General Economic Pressure)
- Inflation, rising import costs, weaker purchasing power affected consumer demand for online shopping, especially for imported / luxury products.
